Web Security

When using the internet, there are several factors to consider in regards to internet safety and web security. Did you know that websites are often vulnerable and subject to security risks? This also applies to networks you connect to when browsing the internet. Unsafe connection and poor website development can lead to loss of private data, stolen information, hacking, cyber attacks, and much more.

"Web security" has two parts, the first is the internal part an the second is the external and public part. When connecting to any public network or even private networks such as your home or work connection, it is important to ensure the website you are accessing is "safe" before entering any information. How can you tell a website connection is private? The website URL will either be completely green, have an "HTTPS" in the beginning of the URL, or there will be a green padlock next to the URL. This indicates that your connection is safe, and that there is a secure connection between your browser and the server

Websites hold substantial amounts of information, from your IP address, your browser status, and even any financial or personal data entered in to the website. With this, it is important to ensure that you have a safe connection and that the code written on a website is bug-free and written to high standards to prevent any of this personal information from leaking.
